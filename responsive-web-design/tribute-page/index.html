NETAJI SUBHASH CHANDRA BOSE

Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose

Here's a time line of Netaji's life:

  • 1897 - Born in Cuttack, Orissa, India.
  • 1916 - Expelled from Presidency College in Calcutta for nationalistic activities.
  • 1919 - He graduated from Scottish Churches College.
  • 1920 - He passed the civil cervice exam after studying at the University of Cambridge.
  • 1921 - Resigned from the Indian Civil Service.
  • 1921 - Resigned from the Indian Civil Service. Sent to jail by the British due to his activities with the Bengal Congress Volunteers in Dec, 1921.
  • 1927 - Upon being released from prison he found the Bengal Congress in disarray.
  • 1936 - Returned from Europe. Upon arriving he was taken into custody and released a year later.
  • 1938 - Elected President of INC and formed a national planning committee, which formulated a policy of broad industrialization. Later in 1939, he resigned due to lack of support from Gandhi.
  • 1940 - Incarcerated Yet Again due to his creation of the Foward Bloc "His refusal to remain in prison at this critical period of India’s history was expressed in a determination to fast to death, which frightened the British government into releasing him.
  • 1941 - Escaped from Prison. Though closely watched, he escaped from is Calcutta residence in disguise and, traveling via Kabul and Moscow, eventually reached Germany in April.
  • 1942 - Began making broadcasts from Germany to South Asia.He and other Indians who had gathered in Berlin made regular broadcasts from the German-sponsored Azad Hind Radio beginning in January 1942, speaking in English, Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Gujarati, and Pashto.
  • 1943 - A little more than a year after the Japanese invasion of Southeast Asia, Bose left Germany, traveling by German and Japanese submarines and by plane, and arrived in May 1943 in Tokyo.
  • 1944 - His Army Reached Indian Soil In a stubborn battle, the mixed Indian and Japanese forces, lacking Japanese air support, were defeated and forced to retreat; the Indian National Army nevertheless for some time succeeded in maintaining its identity as a liberation army, based in Burma and then Indo-China.
  • 1945 - A few days after Japan’s announced surrender in August 1945, Bose, fleeing Southeast Asia, reportedly died in a Japanese hospital in Taiwan as a result of burn injuries from a plane crash.
